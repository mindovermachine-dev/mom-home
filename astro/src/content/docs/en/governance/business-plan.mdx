## About this document

This document constitutes the annex on business operations and financial basis required by the Danish Business Authority when registering a commercial foundation, cf. section 2 of the Danish Commercial Foundations Act.

The document is prepared at the time of establishment and will be updated continuously as the foundation's actual activities evolve.

## Description of business operations

The foundation conducts commercial activity within three focus areas:

**Think Tank** - research, analysis, and dissemination of long-term systems thinking and regenerative principles. Revenue is generated through paid seminars and talks as well as advisory services for organisations and companies.

**Laboratory** - development of software, tools, and methods that demonstrate regenerative principles in practice. Revenue is generated through memberships, compensation for development of primarily Open Source software, consultancy services, and project collaborations with private and public actors.

**Community of Practice** - facilitation of a Community of Practice for practitioners, researchers, students, and decision-makers. Revenue is generated primarily through event tickets and sponsorships from organisations aligned with the foundation's purpose.

Business operations are, as a main rule, conducted on market terms. Pricing is set with reference to prevailing market prices for comparable services. The foundation does not receive public operating subsidies and is not subject to other public supervision than the Danish Business Authority as foundation authority.

## External project financing

The foundation also expects to seek external project financing from private and non-profit foundations for concrete development and research projects.

The target is typically 1-2 concurrent projects with a duration of 12-24 months and support amounts in the range of DKK 0.5-1.5 million per project, depending on project scope, partner constellation, and grant basis.

This financing is not guaranteed, but is a planned and realistic part of the foundation's overall commercial model. Project funding is expected primarily to support laboratory development work, Open Source products, and work that benefits members and collaborators.

Project financing from foundations is expected both as standalone grants and as co-financing of projects where other actors also contribute financially.

## Membership and laboratory content

The foundation's membership model is linked to the laboratory and not to ownership or governance rights.

Memberships finance development of Open Source products, shared roadmap work, technical coordination, and ongoing development for the benefit of members. The membership can be viewed as a "Joint Open Source Roadmap Alliance", where members help fund shared product development, prioritisation, and maintenance.

At the same time, it is a declared intention that the foundation also sells concrete development and projects outside the membership base. Memberships must therefore be seen as part of a broader commercial model and not as the sole economic foundation.

## Leadership and salary assumptions

The founder and CEO is employed from the establishment date and handles day-to-day leadership unless otherwise decided by the board.

The CEO's starting salary is DKK 40,000 per month. The salary is deliberately symbolic in the startup phase and should be seen in light of the foundation's early-stage finances.

The long-term target is a CEO salary of DKK 90,000 per month. No deadline is set for reaching the target salary; this depends solely on the foundation's financial capacity and the board's ongoing assessment.

## Market terms

The foundation's services are offered on open, competitive terms and priced in line with market levels for comparable consultancy, educational, and community services. There is no cross-subsidisation of commercial services from the foundation's non-profit activities.

The foundation aims for a positive contribution margin on commercial activities and for overall financially sustainable operations over time.

Hiring, including CEO salary progression, is carried out only as the foundation's actual earnings, liquidity, and financial resilience make it prudent.

## Salary and hiring assumptions

The foundation is bootstrap-funded in the startup phase. The first significant hiring expansion is expected in the laboratory once there is a sufficient financial basis.

The CEO role is full-time from the date of establishment. Starting salary is DKK 40,000 per month, until the foundation's finances allow gradual progression toward the target salary of DKK 90,000 per month.

No specific deadline is set for salary progression. Progression depends on actual earnings, liquidity, and the board's ongoing assessment of prudent operations.
