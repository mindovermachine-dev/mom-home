---
title: Rules of Procedure for the Board
description: Rules of procedure for the Foundation for Regenerative Software
---

For commercial foundations, it is a legal requirement that the board adopts rules of procedure.

These rules of procedure describe how the board of the Foundation for Regenerative Software carries out its work in practice.

## Purpose and legal basis

These rules of procedure set out the detailed rules for the board's work in the Foundation for Regenerative Software in accordance with section 50 of the Danish Commercial Foundations Act.
The rules of procedure must be interpreted in accordance with the foundation's statutes. In case of conflict, the statutes prevail.
The rules of procedure are reviewed at least once a year and updated as needed.

## Constitution and division of work

The board constitutes itself with a chairperson and vice chairperson in accordance with the statutes.
The chairperson organises, convenes, and chairs board meetings.
The vice chairperson acts in place of the chairperson when needed.
The board defines a clear division of work between the board, the chairperson, any committees, and the executive management.
The board defines in the instructions the framework for establishment, operation, consultation, and nomination rights of the Initiative Circle, cf. [Instructions](/en/governance/instructions/).
If the board assigns special tasks to a board member beyond the board role, this must be decided by the board and recorded.

## Meeting frequency, notice, and decision basis

The board holds the meetings required and at least four ordinary meetings per year.
The chairperson prepares an annual cycle for the board's work, including plans for meetings and recurring topics.
The annual cycle is maintained in the separate document [Annual Cycle](/en/governance/annual-cycle/).
Notice is sent with agenda and relevant materials in due time before the meeting and as a main rule no later than five business days before the meeting.
The board may hold physical, digital, written, and electronic meetings when compatible with its duties.
The board has quorum when more than half of members participate.
A board member may request that an item be added to the agenda.
Decisions are made according to the statutes.
An extraordinary board meeting is held when requested by a board member, executive management, or auditor.
At minimum, the agenda must include formal matters, decision points, reporting from executive management, and follow-up on pending matters.

## Protocol, minutes, and documentation

Decision minutes are maintained for all board meetings.
At minimum, the minutes must state time, participants, conflict of interest matters, decisions, and any dissenting views.
Minutes are approved by the board and stored securely.
Important instructions, policies, and decisions are documented systematically.

## Confidentiality, conflict of interest, and disqualification

Board members, executive management, and any observers are bound by confidentiality regarding confidential matters.
A member may not participate in handling matters where the member has significant personal or financial interests.
Disqualification is clarified at the start of meetings and in relation to specific matters, and the result is recorded.
A disqualified member is not counted in the handling of the relevant matter.

## Executive management, reporting, and oversight

The board oversees executive management's day-to-day leadership.
Executive management reports in a timely, precise, and adequate way to the board on operations, finances, risks, and material dispositions.
The board defines a formal reporting framework, including minimum requirements for periodic management reporting.
The board evaluates executive management's work and the cooperation between board and executive management at least once a year.

## Accounts, internal controls, and audit

The board ensures that bookkeeping and financial reporting are carried out in accordance with legislation and good practice.
The board receives ongoing financial reporting and performs necessary accounting controls.
The board ensures that the auditor has the necessary basis for the audit at all times.
Audit protocols are submitted to the board without undue delay and signed by all board members after review.
At each ordinary board meeting, any new additions in the audit protocol are reviewed.

## Strategy, capital management, distribution policy, and communication

At least once a year, the board addresses the foundation's overall strategy based on the statutes.
The board continuously assesses whether capital management matches the foundation's purpose and needs in the short and long term.
The board defines and revises principles for distribution policy as needed.
The board adopts overall principles for external communication to ensure relevant transparency toward stakeholders.
The chairperson speaks on behalf of the board unless otherwise decided by the board in a specific matter or generally delegated to executive management.
At least once a year, the board identifies the foundation's most significant strategic, operational, and financial reporting risks.

## Self-evaluation

At least once a year, the board carries out an evaluation of the board's work, the chairperson's leadership of board work, and each member's contribution.
The form of evaluation is determined by the board with due regard to the foundation's size and complexity.
Results and any follow-up points are recorded.
At least once a year, the board conducts an ethical review of good foundation governance based on the Committee's reporting template on good foundation governance.

## Selection and nomination of board candidates

The board approves a structured, thorough, and transparent process for selecting and nominating board candidates.
The process must take into account the statutes' rules on nomination rights.
The process and applied criteria are recorded.

When evaluating candidates for the board, whether nominated by the Initiative Circle or through self-supplementation, emphasis is placed on:

- **Professional relevance:** experience or insight within software, software development, technology ethics, sustainability, organisational development, or related fields.
- **Strategic capacity:** ability to contribute to long-term direction and prioritisation rather than solely operational delivery.
- **Integrity and conflict management:** no significant conflicts of interest relative to the foundation's purpose, and willingness to follow the foundation's ethical principles.
- **Diversity:** the board seeks overall breadth in background, perspective, and experience.
- **Capacity:** real time capacity to fulfil the role, including participation in meetings, preparation, and any committee work.

If the board rejects a candidate nominated by the Initiative Circle, rejection must be provided in writing with reasons based on the criteria above or legal disqualification grounds, and communicated to the Initiative Circle no later than 30 days after receiving the nomination.

## The Initiative Circle

The Initiative Circle is the foundation's active community core and may nominate up to 2 candidates to the board according to §§ 5 and 6 of the statutes.

Membership of the Initiative Circle is defined by inclusion in a named, closed communication channel (for example a mailing list, Slack channel, or similar) administered by the foundation's secretariat.

The following objective minimum criteria apply for admission to the Initiative Circle:

- Documented, voluntary contribution to the foundation's Community of Practice within the last 12 months.
- Contributions may include, for example: organising events or meetups, editorial work, professional review, facilitation, Open Source contributions, community coordination, or similar.
- Conflict-of-interest requirements corresponding to those applicable to the board, cf. the section above.

Membership is transparent: the current list of Initiative Circle members is published on an ongoing basis and is publicly available.

Admission criteria are reviewed and published at least once a year by the board.

When the Initiative Circle wishes to nominate a board candidate, the process is:

1. The Initiative Circle makes a nomination decision by simple majority among active contributors.
2. The nomination is communicated in writing to the board with candidate name and a brief rationale.
3. The board confirms receipt and communicates its decision no later than 30 days thereafter.
4. If the Initiative Circle chooses not to nominate candidates in a given period, the board may self-supplement within the framework of the statutes.

The foundation's secretariat supports the Initiative Circle operationally, including meeting notices, minutes, and onboarding of new active contributors. The secretariat does not make decisions on behalf of the Initiative Circle.

## Deputies and other matters

The foundation has no deputy board members unless the board decides to propose a statutory amendment to establish such an arrangement.
The board periodically assesses whether this remains appropriate.

## Entry into force

These rules of procedure enter into force upon adoption by the board.
The rules of procedure are adopted by the board and entered into the board protocol with date.
