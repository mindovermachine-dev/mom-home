## Purpose

The Initiative Circle is the foundation's active community core — an open and self-organizing working community of contributors to the foundation's community of practice.

The Initiative Circle should ensure the foundation's professional and ethical anchoring in the ecosystem and give voice to practice perspectives in the foundation's strategic work.

## Authorization

The framework is based on the foundation's articles of association, including the provisions on the Initiative Circle's advisory role, self-organization and nomination right for up to two board positions.

In the event of a conflict between this document and the articles of association, the articles of association take precedence.

Detailed procedures for operation, consultation and nomination are established in the foundation's rules of procedure, cf. [Rules of Procedure](/en/governance/rules-of-procedure/).

## Role and mandate

The Initiative Circle is advisory and self-organizing — it has no internal decision-making responsibility or management responsibility for the foundation.

The board has full responsibility for the foundation's decisions, supervision and priorities.

The Initiative Circle can make recommendations on strategy, ethics, contribution direction and significant governance issues.

The Initiative Circle can nominate up to two candidates to the board in accordance with the articles of association.

## Membership and establishment

Membership in the Initiative Circle is based on documented, voluntary activity in the foundation's community of practice.

Membership is registered by inclusion in a named, closed communication channel (such as a Slack channel, mailing list or similar) administered by the foundation's secretariat.

Minimum criteria for membership:

- **12-month activity requirement:** Contributions must be documented within the past 12 months
- **Documented contribution:** Active participation may consist of organizing events or meetups, editorial work, professional review, facilitation, contributions to open source, community coordination or similar
- **Integrity:** Members must meet integrity and conflict of interest requirements comparable to the board's own requirements, without significant conflict with the foundation's purpose

Membership is transparent: the current list of Initiative Circle members is published continuously and is available to the public.

## Meetings and working style

The Initiative Circle meets at least once a year, typically as part of the foundation's annual cycle.

Meetings can be held physically or digitally without requirement for physical attendance.

The board or the directors may call for extra consultation sessions on significant strategic or governance issues.

The chair of the board, the directors or an appointed representative participates as a matter of course to ensure consistency between consultation and decision.

The secretariat supports meeting preparation, distribution of materials and minutes — but does not make decisions on behalf of the Initiative Circle.

## Mandatory consultations

The Initiative Circle is consulted before the board adopts significant changes to the articles of association.

The Initiative Circle is consulted before dissolution of the foundation.

The Initiative Circle is consulted before appointment or dismissal of the executive director.

The board may also choose to conduct voluntary consultations in other matters of strategic significance.

## Right of nomination to the board

The Initiative Circle can nominate up to 2 candidates to the board. Nomination is not guaranteed to be accepted — the board makes the final decision.

When the Initiative Circle wishes to nominate a candidate:

1. The Initiative Circle makes a decision on nomination by simple majority among active members
2. The nomination is communicated to the board in writing with the candidate's name and a brief justification
3. The board confirms receipt and communicates its decision within 30 days
4. If the board rejects the candidate, the rejection must be in writing and justified on the basis of documented, objective grounds (disqualification, conflict of interest, failure to meet published criteria)

Candidates are assessed against the board's overall competence needs, integrity and statutory requirements, cf. [Rules of Procedure](/en/governance/rules-of-procedure/).

The nomination is recorded and included in the board's overall documentation of the appointment process.

## Integrity, confidentiality and documentation

Members of the Initiative Circle must disclose matters that may create conflicts of interest in specific cases.

Confidential information is treated with the same care as in the foundation's other governance work.

A meeting note or consultation document is prepared after each meeting with a clear indication of recommendations and any points of disagreement.

## Relationship to annual cycle

The Initiative Circle's annual meeting is planned as part of the foundation's annual cycle, cf. [Annual Cycle](/en/governance/annual-cycle/).

Results from consultations and the annual meeting are processed by the board at the next ordinary meeting and translated into concrete follow-up points.

## Annual review

The board evaluates at least once a year whether the Initiative Circle functions according to its purpose and provides sufficient value in the decision-making process.

This document is updated as needed and at least once a year.

The criteria for membership in the Initiative Circle are reviewed and published at least once a year by the board.
