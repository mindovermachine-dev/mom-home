## About this document

The statutes are the foundation's constitution. They define the foundation's purpose, governance structure, and legal framework.

This page publishes the current statute text so it is easily accessible to the public.

## Name and registered office

**§ 1.**

Subsection 1. The foundation's name is "Mind over Machine, the foundation for promoting regenerative software development".

Subsection 3. The foundation's registered office is the Municipality of Copenhagen.

## Founder

**§ 2.**

Subsection 1. The foundation is established jointly by:

- The association DevOpsDays Copenhagen (CVR: 39299127)
- KruseControl (CVR: 25317459)

Subsection 2. The foundation is established through contribution of the founding capital and as a continuation of the visions of the founding entities.

## Purpose

**§ 3.**

Subsection 1. The foundation's overall purpose is to explore, investigate, and disseminate models and practices for how software and software development can contribute to a regenerative paradigm that creates positive value for people, society, and the world.

Subsection 2. The foundation assumes that the term "regenerative" in the context of software and software development means that both product and process are restorative, healing, or rebuilding.

Subsection 3. The foundation operates within a socio-technological discourse and agenda focused on the ethical and philosophical meeting between humans and machines, as well as on quality in both software products and processes.

Subsection 4. The purpose is pursued through three focus areas:

- Think tank: research, analysis, and dissemination of long-term systems thinking and regenerative principles within disciplines related to or derived from software and software development.
- Laboratory: development of software, tools, and methods that demonstrate regenerative principles in practice.
- Community of Practice: facilitation of a Community of Practice for practitioners, researchers, students, and decision-makers aimed at advancing regenerative software, software development, and technology understanding.

Subsection 5. The foundation is commercially operating and may conduct business directly or indirectly related to its purpose.

## The foundation's capital and special rights

**§ 4.**

Subsection 1. The foundation's founding capital amounts to DKK 300,000. The capital is contributed in cash jointly by the founders.

Subsection 2. No special rights or benefits are granted to the founders or others.

## Board of Directors

**§ 5.**

Subsection 1. The foundation is managed by a board of 3-5 members.

Subsection 2. Board members are appointed for a period of 2 years. Reappointment is possible.

Subsection 3. Board appointments are made as follows:

- The Initiative Circle may nominate up to 2 candidates for the board, cf. § 6 subsection 3.
- The remaining members are appointed by the sitting board (self-supplementation) based on professional, ethical, and otherwise relevant qualities.

Subsection 4. The board constitutes itself with a chairperson and a vice chairperson.

Subsection 5. The board may reject a candidate nominated by the Initiative Circle if there is an objective and documented reason, including legal disqualification, significant conflict of interest in relation to the foundation's purpose, or failure to meet objective requirements laid down in the board's rules of procedure. Rejection must be provided in writing with reasons no later than 30 days after receiving the nomination.

## Initiative Circle

**§ 6.**

Subsection 1. The foundation has an Initiative Circle to ensure professional and ethical anchoring in the Community of Practice.

Subsection 2. The Initiative Circle is an open and self-organising working community of active contributors from the foundation's Community of Practice.

Subsection 3. The Initiative Circle functions as an advisory body for the foundation's strategic direction and may nominate up to 2 candidates for the board in accordance with § 5 subsection 3.

Subsection 4. Meetings and other activities in the Initiative Circle may be conducted digitally without a requirement for physical attendance.

Subsection 5. The board must consult the Initiative Circle before decisions on amendments to the statutes, dissolution of the foundation, and appointment or dismissal of the CEO. The detailed process for consultation and nomination is laid down in the foundation's instructions and rules of procedure.

## Board work and executive management

**§ 7.**

Subsection 1. The board holds the strategic management of the foundation. Decisions are made by simple majority unless otherwise follows from § 11 subsection 1. In case of a tie, the chairperson has the casting vote.

Subsection 2. The board appoints a CEO to manage the day-to-day operation of the foundation's units (Think Tank, Laboratory, and Community of Practice). As a main rule, the CEO participates ex officio, without voting rights, in all board meetings unless the board decides otherwise in a specific case.

Subsection 3. Board meetings may be held using electronic media (digital meetings), if the chairperson finds this appropriate and compatible with the board's duties.

Subsection 4. In collaboration with the CEO, the board sets the detailed framework, conditions, and intentions for work in the foundation's focus areas (§ 3) in internal instructions or rules of procedure.

## Accounts and audit

**§ 8.**

Subsection 1. The foundation's financial year runs from 1 October to 30 September.

Subsection 2. The first accounting period runs from the date of establishment to 30 September 2027.

Subsection 3. The annual accounts must be audited by a state-authorized or registered auditor.

## Use of surplus and distributions

**§ 9.**

Subsection 1. After necessary allocations for consolidation, the foundation's surplus must be used to advance the foundation's purpose, cf. § 3.

Subsection 2. The board decides on distributions to projects, research, or initiatives that promote a deeper understanding of the regenerative paradigm.

## Signature rule

**§ 10.**

Subsection 1. The foundation is bound by the full board, by the chairperson jointly with another board member, or by the chairperson jointly with a CEO.

## Amendments to statutes and dissolution

**§ 11.**

Subsection 1. Amendments to the statutes require unanimity on the board and approval by the foundation authority. The Initiative Circle must be consulted before material changes.

Subsection 2. Upon dissolution, the foundation's remaining assets must be used for non-profit purposes within socio-technological research or regenerative technology, as determined by the board and approved by the foundation authority.
