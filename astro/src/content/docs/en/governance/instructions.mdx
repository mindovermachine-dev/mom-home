## Purpose

This document gathers the instructions that govern the foundation's daily operations.

The ambition is simple: the public should be able to see how we actually work, and internally we should have clear, practical frameworks for decisions, responsibility, and quality.

The instructions are therefore written in readable language. They are operational, but should also be readable as an open narrative about how the foundation is governed.

## The Board

The board has strategic responsibility for the foundation's direction, capital, and integrity. This means that board work is not only about control, but about keeping the course aligned with purpose, even when day-to-day pressures pull in other directions.

The board works according to an annual cycle with fixed themes: strategy, distribution direction, capital management, risks, evaluation, and public reporting. Meetings are organised so decisions can be made on an informed basis, and so follow-up is a real part of governance rather than a formality.

The specific rhythm and fixed deliveries are described in [Annual Cycle](/en/governance/annual-cycle/).

The board also maintains separate instructions for the Initiative Circle as an advisory consultation body, including processes for consultation and nomination rights, cf. [Statutes](/en/governance/statutes/).

When appointing new members, the board uses an overall assessment of competencies, continuity, renewal, and diversity. Independence is assessed and documented annually so it is transparent which ties may exist and how they are handled.

The board publishes relevant annual information on composition, remuneration, and key governance matters in accordance with applicable rules and good foundation governance.

## The Initiative Circle

The Initiative Circle is the active core of the foundation's Community of Practice. The core is self-organising and consists of contributors who take on concrete tasks such as events, meetup activities, editorial work, review, facilitation, or professional coordination.

The purpose of the Initiative Circle is to create professional anchoring close to practice and provide a clear yet simple channel for input and nominations to the board.

Establishment and maintenance follow these principles:

- The Initiative Circle is open to active contributors in the Community of Practice.
- The board sets objective minimum criteria for active participation, published and reviewed at least once a year.
- The Initiative Circle constitutes itself and may continuously admit new active contributors.
- The Initiative Circle may nominate up to 2 candidates to the board according to the procedures in statutes and rules of procedure.
- If the Initiative Circle chooses not to nominate candidates in a given period, the board may self-supplement within the statutes.

To prevent volunteer overload, the Initiative Circle is supported by the foundation's secretariat:

- The secretariat handles practical coordination, meeting notices, minutes, and follow-up.
- The secretariat supports onboarding of new active contributors.
- The secretariat facilitates the work but does not make decisions on behalf of the Initiative Circle.

The concrete work rhythm, deadlines, and documentation requirements are defined in a separate internal process instruction.

## Executive Management

Executive management leads day-to-day operations within the framework established by statutes, rules of procedure, and board decisions. The role is both operational and committed to clear reporting.

Executive management is responsible for ensuring that operations, finances, quality, and internal controls are sound. Significant or extraordinary dispositions are submitted to the board, and in urgent situations a fixed practice is followed for immediate notification and subsequent treatment.

Executive management reports regularly on status, risks, significant dispositions, and expectations for the upcoming period. Reporting is intended not only to inform but to make board oversight effective.

## Employees

Employees work within a clear framework of professional quality, ethical responsibility, and documented practice. This applies to development work, collaboration with stakeholders, and internal knowledge sharing.

Employees are expected to act loyally in relation to the foundation's purpose, protect confidential information, and raise issues that may challenge quality, legal compliance, or ethical integrity.

Work is organised so learning, improvement, and responsibility go hand in hand. This includes documenting significant decisions, using mistakes constructively for improvement, and ensuring clear roles and expectations in the cooperation between employees, executive management, and board.

## Future Vision: Organisation at Growth

When the foundation hires its first employee, we will gradually implement principles for transparent and sustainable leadership. This section describes the values and practices we intend to establish when building organisational culture.

Employees should have both professional quality and a financial interest in the foundation's success. We will work with:

- **Transparent competence and salary structure:** clear level and role categories with known salary information, based on market conditions and internal fairness.
- **Mentorship model:** each employee receives an experienced colleague as mentor, inspired by _senpai-kohai_ principles, to ensure professional growth and a clear path to advancement.
- **Economic participation:** when the foundation's finances allow, employees will receive a share of annual surplus through a bonus model, ensuring ownership of results.
- **Annual evaluation:** meeting between employee, mentor, and executive management on development, wellbeing, and possible role or level changes.

These principles will be formalised in internal HR documents and evaluated annually as part of the foundation's ethical review.

## Annual Update

The instructions are reviewed at least once a year in connection with the foundation's ethical assessment and annual report.

When changes are made, the document is updated with a clear rationale so internal and external stakeholders can follow the development of foundation governance.
