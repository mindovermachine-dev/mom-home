Machines are a means of expression, just like language, music, dance, sports or architecture. In fact, software is 'literally' a language to instruct computers, in various abstractions of their core mechanism (binary logic, unless you have a quantum or bionic processor). If you thought "over Machine" in our name was meant to be a contest, think again: how are we speaking our Mind to you right now?

As the most ubiquitous platform of our time, the language of the Web is of primary concern for us. We use it to deliver static text, images, audio and video, but can we "regenerate" this content, or its infrastructure if needed? The one depicted as below by Randall Munroe's "Dependency" meme in 2020, and a latest iteration of it recently. We have concerns. Tim Berners-Lee [has concerns](https://www.newyorker.com/magazine/2025/10/06/tim-berners-lee-invented-the-world-wide-web-now-he-wants-to-save-it). And the European Commission has [mandates](https://digital-strategy.ec.europa.eu/en/policies/cyber-resilience-act), effective 9/11 this year.

Mind over Machine is opening its WEB LAB to lead the way to a more resilient future, exercising our proposed [standards of quality](https://docs.mindovermachine.dk/da/methodology/how-we-work/), those of the [Open Science Framework](https://www.cos.io/open-science), the W3C's [foundational specifications](https://www.w3.org/DesignIssues/), and actual implementations of open source web content and infrastructure as a [Community of Practice](https://www.communityofpractice.ca/background/why-communities-of-practice-are-important/) \- from servers through client-side applications to [browsers](https://ladybird.org/). Working hands-on, but from first principles, beginners and experts alike are welcome. By week three we anticipate a [ring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Webring) of personal websites and/or project prototypes\! Come join if you would like to pioneer the distributed web\!
